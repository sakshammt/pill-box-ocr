# 📸 OCR Accuracy Guide - Getting Better Results

## Why OCR Might Not Extract Names Correctly

OCR (Optical Character Recognition) accuracy depends on several factors. Here's what affects it and how to improve results.

---

## 🔍 Common Issues & Solutions

### Issue 1: Poor Lighting ☀️

**Problem**: Text is too dark, has shadows, or glare  
**Solution**:
- Use natural daylight when possible
- Turn on room lights
- Avoid direct flash (causes glare)
- Avoid shadows on the medicine label
- Position the medicine near a window

**Best Time**: Daytime with indirect sunlight

---

### Issue 2: Blurry Images 📷

**Problem**: Camera not focused, hand shaking  
**Solution**:
- Hold your phone/camera steady (use both hands)
- Wait for autofocus to complete
- Tap on the text area to focus (on phones)
- Rest your hand on a surface for stability
- Take multiple photos and use the clearest one

**Pro Tip**: Count to 3 before pressing the capture button

---

### Issue 3: Text Too Small 🔍

**Problem**: Medicine label is too far away  
**Solution**:
- Get MUCH closer to the text
- Fill the entire frame with just the medicine name
- The name should be large and readable
- Zoom in if your camera allows
- For small text, use a magnifying glass + camera

**Ideal Distance**: 10-20 cm (4-8 inches) from the label

---

### Issue 4: Angle & Perspective 📐

**Problem**: Photo taken at an angle  
**Solution**:
- Hold camera parallel to the label (not tilted)
- Look straight at the text, not from the side
- Ensure the label is flat (not curved around bottle)
- For bottles, rotate to see the flattest part
- Avoid perspective distortion

**Best Angle**: 90° perpendicular to the label

---

### Issue 5: Background Clutter 🎨

**Problem**: Too much information in the image  
**Solution**:
- Crop to show ONLY the medicine name
- Remove other text from the frame
- Use a plain background (white paper works great)
- Cover other text with your hand
- Focus on the largest text (usually the name)

**Best Practice**: Medicine name only, nothing else

---

### Issue 6: Font & Text Quality 🔤

**Problem**: Small fonts, fancy fonts, or faded text  
**Solution**:
- Look for the clearest text on the package
- Front of box usually has clearest name
- Avoid handwritten labels
- Avoid prescription stickers (use box instead)
- Prefer printed labels over handwritten ones

**If text is faded**: Try to find a newer package or use manual entry

---

## 🎯 Best Practices for 90%+ Accuracy

### ✅ The Perfect OCR Photo:

1. **Good Lighting** - Bright, even, no shadows
2. **Close Distance** - Fill the frame with text
3. **Sharp Focus** - Wait for autofocus
4. **Straight Angle** - Camera parallel to label
5. **Plain Background** - Minimal clutter
6. **Clear Text** - Largest, clearest medicine name

### 📱 Step-by-Step Perfect Capture:

```
1. Find the medicine name (usually largest text on box)
2. Place medicine on a flat surface with good light
3. Hold phone directly above, parallel to label
4. Get close (10-20 cm away)
5. Tap on the medicine name to focus
6. Wait for autofocus (text should be sharp)
7. Hold steady and capture
8. Review - text should be clear and readable
```

## 💡 Pro Tips from Experience

The front of medicine boxes usually has:
- Largest text
- Clearest printing
- Brand name prominently displayed
- Better contrast

### Tip 2: Natural Light is Best
- Morning or afternoon sunlight
- Near a window (not direct sun)
- Overcast days work great
- Avoid yellow indoor lighting if possible

### Tip 3: Multiple Attempts
- Take 2-3 photos
- Try different angles

- Upload the clearest one

Many phones have:
- Digital zoom (use sparingly)
- Macro mode (for close-ups)
- Portrait mode (background blur)

### Tip 5: Clean the Label
- Wipe dust off the label
- Smooth out wrinkled labels
- Flatten curved labels with your hand

## 🔧 Troubleshooting Specific Cases

### Case 1: Medicine Names in Multiple Languages

**Problem**: Label has English + another language  
**Solution**:
- Cover the non-English text
- Crop to show only English name
- Our OCR is optimized for English text

### Case 2: Very Small Print

**Problem**: Generic medicine, tiny text  
**Solution**:
- Use phone's zoom/macro mode
- Get VERY close
- Use a magnifying glass
- Take photo through magnifying glass
- Or just type it manually (faster!)

### Case 3: Glossy/Reflective Packaging

**Problem**: Glare from camera flash/light  
**Solution**:
- Turn off flash
- Tilt package slightly to avoid reflection
- Use indirect lighting (bounce light off wall)
- Change angle slightly

### Case 4: Faded or Worn Labels

**Problem**: Old medicine, text rubbed off  
**Solution**:
- Look for unused backup box
- Check the bottle insert (paper inside)
- Check pharmacy receipt
- Manual entry is best option

### Case 5: Handwritten Prescriptions

**Problem**: Doctor's handwriting unclear  
**Solution**:
- OCR won't work well on handwriting
- Use the medicine box instead
- Or type the name manually
- Ask pharmacist for printed label

## 🎓 Understanding OCR Limitations

### OCR is Good At:
- ✅ Printed text
- ✅ Clear fonts
- ✅ High contrast (black on white)
- ✅ Straight, flat text
- ✅ Standard sizes

### OCR Struggles With:
- ❌ Handwriting
- ❌ Cursive/script fonts
- ❌ Very small text (< 10pt)
- ❌ Low contrast (gray on white)
- ❌ Curved/distorted text
- ❌ Artistic/decorative fonts

**That's why manual editing is available!**
